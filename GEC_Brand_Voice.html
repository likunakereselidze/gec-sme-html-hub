1 · Brand Identity Who GEC is and how it speaks
GEC in one sentence

GEC is the only institution in Georgia that takes an SME owner from where they are and builds the structure, skills, and network needed to grow — not through theory, but through practitioners who have done it themselves.

  • Voice
    Warm but professional. Speaks like a smart colleague — not a lecturer, not a brand robot. Uses "you" (შენ/თქვენ) directly.
  • Tone
    Energetic and practical. GEC celebrates what's possible. Posts motivate — they don't preach.
  • Authority
    Comes from the practitioners and track record — not corporate language or statistics alone.
  • Community
    GEC sees SME owners as a peer community. Posts say "ერთად" (together) and "ჩვენ" (we/us) — it's not GEC talking down to business owners.
  • Never
    GEC never sounds bureaucratic, distant, or like a government agency. No passive voice. No jargon-heavy academic tone.
2 · Post Structure The 5-part formula GEC uses
Key rule: Every GEC post is bilingual — full Georgian text first, then ― (em-dash divider), then full English text. Never mix languages in the same block.

Part 1 — Hook (1–2 lines)

Opens with a question, a problem, or a bold statement. Always emoji-led. Should stop the scroll.

🎯 თქვენი ბიზნესი ზრდა შეწყვიტა — მაგრამ არ იცით რატომ? 👉 ეს გავრცელებული სცენარია. და მას სახელი აქვს.

Part 2 — Problem Unpacking (3–5 short lines)

Describes the problem in simple, relatable language. Short sentences. Often bullet-style with emojis.

⚠️ მენეჯმენტი — ყველაფერი თქვენზეა მიბმული ⚠️ გეგმა — თავში გაქვთ, ქაღალდზე არ არის ⚠️ ინვესტიციაზე ლაპარაკობენ — მაგრამ ბიზნესი მზად არ არის

Part 3 — Solution Bridge (2–3 lines)

How GEC / this program addresses it. Specific, not generic. Mentions mentors, weeks, cohort.

✅ GEC SME განვითარების პროგრამა — 40 კვირა, 13 პრაქტიკოსი, 20 კომპანია. სტრუქტურა, რომელიც ბიზნესს ბიზნესისთვის მუშაობს.

Part 4 — Program Details (compact)

Cohort, dates, spots, registration window. Always on its own line for scannability.

📅 კოჰორტი 1 · 11 ივნისი, 2026 🗓 რეგისტრაცია: 5–31 მაისი · ინტერვიუ: 1–5 ივნისი 🏷 20 ადგილი · 20 კომპანია

Part 5 — CTA

Clear next action. Link + phone + website. Short and direct — no passive "feel free to..."

3 · Language Rules How to write Georgian and English correctly

Georgian

  • Register
    Formal but warm. Use თქვენ (formal you) for addressing business owners. Not too stiff.
  • Sentences
    Short and punchy. One idea per sentence. Rhetorical questions work well as hooks.
  • Verbs
    Active. Use action verbs: შემოდით, გამოიყენეთ, გაიარეთ, მიიღეთ — not passive constructions.
  • Numbers
    Use digits, not spelled-out words: 20 ადგილი, 40 კვირა, 13 მენტორი
  • Avoid
    Literary prose, long subordinate clauses, academic vocabulary. GEC posts are not essays — they are social copy.
  • Avoid
    "ლამაზი", "ღირებული", "ინოვაციური" — vague flattery words. Replace with specific facts.

English

  • Register
    Direct and confident. Speaks to a business owner as a peer. Second person: "you", "your business".
  • Sentences
    Short. No dependent-clause chains. Max 20 words per sentence.
  • Specificity
    Name numbers, outcomes, credentials — no vague promises. "40 weeks, 13 mentors" beats "intensive program".
  • Avoid
    British over-formality: "Please note that...", "We would like to inform you..." — cut these entirely.
  • Avoid
    Long editorial paragraphs. The English block mirrors the Georgian: scannable, punchy, action-oriented.

7 · Visual Specs Carousel slides, colors, layout rules
Format: GEC posts use carousel slides (1080×1080px). Slide 1 = hook/headline. Slides 2–4 = details/bullets. Last slide = CTA + logo + tagline.

Brand Colors

#E8471C — GEC Orange (headlines on slides)
#153749 — Navy (backgrounds, body text)
#226263 — Teal (accents, dividers)
#F0B91C — Gold (highlights, badges)
#FFFFFF — White (text on dark slides)

Slide Layout Rules

  • GEC logo always top-left corner
  • Tagline bottom of last slide: "From Delivery to Lasting Impact"
  • Orange for bold keywords / headline
  • White text on navy/dark background
  • No more than 6 lines of text per slide
  • Teal strip or line as visual accent
  • No stock photos — geometric/abstract preferred
  • www.gecbusiness.com on every CTA slide

Typography

  • Headline: Bold, 36–48px, orange or white
  • Body: Regular/Medium, 18–22px, white or dark navy
  • Caption: 14–16px, muted gray
  • Font: System / sans-serif (Montserrat or similar)

Carousel Slide Order

  • Slide 1: Hook headline (orange text)
  • Slide 2: Problem bullets
  • Slide 3: Solution / what program offers
  • Slide 4: Mentor names or key stats
  • Slide 5: CTA — dates, spots, link, logo
